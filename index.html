Thinking of Adopting a Cat?

Check out these helpful tips, gathered by American Humane.

  1. Adopt Two Cats: Cats need exercise, mental stimulation, and social interaction. Two cats can provide this for each other and offer additional benefits, like their soothing purring.
  2. Match Personalities: Cats have unique personalities. Long-haired, round-bodied cats are usually laid-back, while short-haired, narrow-bodied cats are more active.
  3. Prepare a Veterinarian Visit: Schedule a vet visit soon after adoption and bring the cat's medical records.
  4. Prepare Your Household: Ensure everyone at home is ready for the cat. Discuss with shelters about introducing the new cat to existing pets.
  5. Budget Responsibly: Factor in the cost of care, including food, supplies, and medical expenses.
  6. Stock Up on Supplies: Essentials include litter box, litter, bowls, food, scratching posts, toys, grooming tools, and a bed.
  7. Cat-Proof Your Home: Remove hazards like loose items, electric cords, and small objects kittens might swallow.
  8. Introduce Slowly: Give the cat time to adjust to its new environment, ideally starting in one room.
  9. Include Cats in Emergency Plans: Ensure your emergency plan includes pets, with contact numbers for a vet and 24-hour animal hospital.
  10. Avoid Surprise Gifts: If gifting a cat, involve the recipient in the process to ensure compatibility and commitment.
